# **🌌 Infinite Life Simulator — Ultimate Modding & CG Guide**

Welcome to the official modding documentation for **Infinite Life Simulator (Try A Life)**.

This engine is not just a text-based RPG; it is a **Multimedia Visual Novel Engine powered by LLMs (Large Language Models)**. As a modder, you have the power to create entirely new universes—from gritty cyberpunk dystopias to magical academies—and orchestrate cinematic video playbacks (CGs) based on AI-generated narratives.

## **📂 1\. The Architecture of a Mod**

A complete, immersive Mod consists of two main components:

1. **The Brain (.json)**: The lore, rules, and logic that guide the AI.  
2. **The Visuals (.ogv)**: The cinematic videos played during critical life events.

To start modding, your files must be placed exactly like this in the game directory:

Try\_A\_Life\_Game/  
├── backend/world\_data/worlds/  
│   └── your\_world\_name.json       \<-- \[The Brain\] Your world config file  
└── mods/  
    └── your\_world\_name/           \<-- \[The Visuals\] Folder name MUST match the json name  
        └── cgs/  
            ├── epic\_battle.ogv    \<-- Your converted video files  
            └── tragic\_death.ogv

## **🧠 2\. Forging The Lore: JSON Configuration**

The .json file is the prompt blueprint that restricts and guides the LLM. You can use backend/world\_data/worlds/diy\_example.json as your template.

### **2.1 Core World Definition (info & attributes)**

* **info.rules**: This is the most critical field. Define the physical and social laws. (e.g., *"Magic requires wands. The dark arts are strictly forbidden and punished by death."*)  
* **attributes.display\_names**: You cannot add or remove the 7 core stats, but you MUST rename them to fit your lore.  
  * *Example*: Rename constitution to Vitality, wealth to Galleons, willpower to Sanity.

### **2.2 Designing the Lifespan (age\_constraints & age\_themes)**

The AI needs to know what is appropriate for a 5-year-old versus a 50-year-old.

* **age\_constraints**: Hard limits. (e.g., "0-10": "Confined to the village, cannot fight monsters.")  
* **age\_themes**: Micro-story seeds. The AI will draw inspiration from these to generate daily events.  
  * *Pro-Tip*: Be incredibly specific and visual.  
  * ❌ Bad: *"Something bad happens at school."*  
  * ✅ Good: *"The senior disciples ambush you in the alchemy lab, shattering your cauldron."*

### **2.3 Orchestrating Boss Fights (node\_ages & node\_templates)**

"Nodes" are life-or-death tribulations.

* Define the exact ages they occur: "node\_ages": \[18, 30, 50\]  
* Write the setup prompt in "node\_templates". The AI will use this as the opening scene for the tribulation before asking the player to sacrifice stats.

## **🎬 3\. The Visual Engine: Triggering CGs**

You can trigger full-screen cinematic videos when the AI generates specific keywords. This is controlled by the "cg\_triggers" array in your JSON file.

### **How Keyword Sniffing Works**

When an event resolves, the engine scans the AI's output text. If it finds a keyword, it triggers the assigned video.

"cg\_triggers": \[  
  {  
    "\_comment": "Triggers during a Tribulation (node) if 'Dragon' is mentioned.",  
    "keywords": \["Dragon", "Firebreath", "Scales"\],  
    "type": "node",  
    "success\_video": "slay\_dragon.ogv",  
    "fail\_video": "burnt\_to\_ash.ogv"  
  },  
  {  
    "\_comment": "Triggers during normal events.",  
    "keywords": \["Graduation", "University"\],  
    "type": "normal",  
    "success\_video": "throw\_hats.ogv"  
  }  
\]

## **⚠️ 4\. CRITICAL: Video Encoding Rules (Godot Hardware Reality)**

The Godot 4 engine's native video player relies heavily on CPU decoding. To prevent the game from crashing, lagging, or displaying a "glitched snow" screen, **YOU MUST STRICTLY FOLLOW THESE RULES**:

### **Rule 1: Format MUST be Ogg Theora (.ogv)**

MP4, AVI, MKV, or WebM (VP9) are **NOT** natively supported.

### **Rule 2: The Macroblock Alignment Rule (Divisible by 16\)**

The Theora decoder calculates pixels in 16x16 blocks. **Your video's width and height MUST be evenly divisible by 16\.**

* ✅ **Supported & Recommended**: 1280x720 (720p)  
* ❌ **Will Crash/Glitch**: 1920x1080 (Too heavy for CPU), 1276x664 (Not divisible by 16).

### **Rule 3: Strip the Audio**

The game engine automatically crossfades the Background Music (BGM) when a CG plays. Built-in audio in your video will clash with the engine and consume unnecessary CPU power. **Always export videos without an audio track.**

### **🛠️ The Ultimate Conversion Method (FFmpeg)**

The easiest and highest-quality way to convert your .mp4 generated by AI (like Runway, Kling, or Midjourney) is using FFmpeg via the command line.

1. Download [FFmpeg Windows Builds](https://www.gyan.dev/ffmpeg/builds/) (ffmpeg-release-essentials.zip).  
2. Extract the .zip and open the bin folder.  
3. Place your input.mp4 into the bin folder.  
4. Open Command Prompt in that folder and run this exact command:

ffmpeg \-i input.mp4 \-vf scale=1280:720 \-c:v libtheora \-q:v 8 \-an output.ogv

**What this magic command does:**

* \-vf scale=1280:720: Forces a perfect 720p resize (fixes all macroblock glitches).  
* \-c:v libtheora: Converts to Godot's required Theora codec.  
* \-q:v 8: Sets the visual quality to near-lossless (10 is max, 8 is the sweet spot for performance).  
* \-an: Strips the audio track to unburden the Godot engine.

## **⚖️ 5\. Advanced Modding Tips**

### **Guarding Immersion with forbidden\_patterns**

LLMs occasionally hallucinate modern concepts into historical or fantasy settings. Use Regex patterns to ban them:

"forbidden\_patterns": \[  
    "phone", "internet", "car\\\\b", "gun\\\\b", "social media", "AI", "robot"  
\]

### **Balancing the Difficulty**

Adjust "reward\_limits" to set the tone of your world:

* **Grimdark Survival**: max\_reward: 3, max\_penalty: \-10, hp\_max\_penalty: \-20 (High mortality rate).  
* **Cozy Slice of Life**: max\_reward: 5, max\_penalty: \-3, hp\_max\_penalty: \-5 (Very safe).  
* **Shonen Anime / Cultivation**: max\_reward: 8, max\_penalty: \-8 (High risk, massive rewards).
